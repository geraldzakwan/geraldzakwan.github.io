P5 Virtual Glove

An input device for human–computer interaction worn like a glove

P5 test P5 Virtual Glove adalah sebuah perangkat masukan (input) yang mendeteksi posisi relatif dan gerakan jari dari tangan pemakai. Perangkat ini mendukung interaksi dalam lingkungan tiga dimensi guna meningkatkan pengalaman pemakai dan menciptakan feel yang mendekati nyata saat pemakai mengoperasikan perangkat ini.

Perangkat ini memang belum digunakan secara masif. Namun, pada umumnya perangkat ini sudah banyak digunakan dalam dunia gaming oleh para gamer profesional di seluruh dunia. Selain digunakan dalam dunia gaming, perangkat ini juga digunakan sebagai media edukasi dan visualisasi tiga dimensi.

CBT in Indonesia

A computer-assisted test used mainly in educational and governmental enviroment of Indonesia

Terdapat dua sistem pengujian/tes yang digunakan di Indonesia baik di lingkungan pendidikan maupun pemerintahan, yakni PBT (paper-based test) dan CBT (computer-based test).

Sistem PBT adalah sistem yang konvensional. Sistem ini lazim digunakan di dunia pendidikan seperti dalam penyelenggaraan ujian nasional (UN) untuk tingkat SD, SMP, maupun SMA. Peserta ujian akan diberikan lembar soal dan lembar jawaban. Soal berbentuk pilihan ganda dan lembar jawaban menyediakan pilihan jawaban dalam bentuk bulatan-bulatan untuk kemudian dihitamkan oleh peserta. Pemeriksaan dilakukan oleh scanner secara otomatis terhadap bulatan yang dihitamkan peserta. Selain untuk UN, PBT juga digunakan dalam penyelenggaraan seleksi masuk perguruan tinggi (SBMPTN) dan seleksi pegawai negeri sipil (tes CPNS). Tes lain seperti TOEFL dan IELTS yang diselenggarakan lembaga pengajaran bahasa Inggris juga umumnya masih berbasis PBT.

Namun, beberapa tahun ke belakang, pemerintah Indonesia mulai mencanangkan program CBT dan mengenalkannya pada berbagai kalangan. Dalam dunia pendidikan misalnya, ujian nasional dengan CBT mulai diselenggarakan pada tahun 2015 lalu. Meski cakupannya masih sedikit, namun ada manfaat dan keuntungan yang dirasakan menurut pengalaman beberapa siswa yang mengikuti UN dengan sistem CBT. Keuntungannya adalah siswa tidak perlu lagi menghabiskan waktu untuk membulatkan pilihan di lembar jawaban dengan teliti. Pada sistem CBT, siswa cukup meng-klik pilihan jawaban pada layar komputer. Selain itu, siswa tidak perlu takut lembar jawabannya tercecer. Sistem CBT akan secara otomatis mengirimkan hasil pekerjaan siswa secara digital. Hal ini tentu memberikan rasa tenang bagi siswa baik saat ujian maupun sesudah ujian terutama pada pelaksanaan SBMPTN yang menentukan nasib kelanjutan pendidikan siswa.

Implementation

Connecting the dots between P5 Virtual Glove and CBT system

We've seen that CBT is much more efficient than PBT. But, how about increasing even more of CBT efficiency?

We know that in CBT, participant can easily choose the answer by clicking on the appropriate button. They can also navigate between the questions easily by clicking on a next and previous button. But, I think there is a problem there. Let’s take example of CBT for national exam of mathematics. Most of the students use their right hand for both writing and using a computer mouse. So, they have to move their right hand every time they have finished writing the solution for the question and want to grab a mouse for choosing the answer on the monitor. Even though that sounds like it is just a little problem, but it is actually a time waste as they have to switch their hand between pencil to mouse for every question in the exam. Sometimes if the mouse position is a little bit far from the table position for writing then they have to move their body as well.

So, why don’t we use a virtual glove? Participants can use a virtual glove for their left hand. So, their right hand can be mainly used for writing and their left hand can handle the rest. It is cool to navigate each question by swiping your hand left and right. It is cool as well to choose an answer by simply move a single finger. As we human have five fingers, then that is enough to hold five choices from A to E. So, this is my idea to increase CBT system even more efficiently by using P5 virtual glove. The detail implementations are on the next paragraphs.

Basically, to start solving this problem, we have to create a standard system for CBT. We have to make the question appears on an interface that can accept keystroke for navigating questions and choosing answer. Let’s take a very example interface which is suitable and capable of doing that requirement. We can simply start using a Java Applet which has KeyListener feature on it. For further development, any similar frameworks with better capabilities, looks, and performances are considered.

Then, we have to do the settings on P5 Glove Control Panel. We need to navigate to the button tab and do settings for each action. For example, if you bend your thumb then the computer will take it as ‘A’ button keystroke (the computer will take the action just like a keyboard hit for ‘A’ button). The settings preference is up to the user, the default will be thumb for ‘A’, index finger for ‘B’, middle finger for ‘C’, ring finger for ‘D’, and pinkie for ‘E’. The settings can be done using either Essential Reality's driver or Dual Mode driver. I don't know whether there is a setting mechanism which allows me to receive a keystroke directly from a glove action such as bending finger or rotating/moving hand position. If there is none, maybe I have to develop an interface for this or simply use P5 Glove normally. By normally I mean you can just move the cursor on screen by your hand and click on the answer by bending your index finger (or other finger, you can set preference for this and I make sure this mechanism will work just fine).

Another option is using virtual glove designed for gaming. If I use Peregrine glove which is designed for gaming, it would have been easier for me and can even trigger more keystrokes. To trigger keystroke in Peregrine, you need to hit your finger on specific location. There will be five segments on each of your finger except thumb and pinkie. Your pinkie will have three segments and your thumb will be used to hit the segment. By hitting each segment, you will trigger a unique keystroke. So, there will be 18 keystrokes you can trigger using a single glove. You can also set preference keystroke for each segment in Peregrine settings.

The settings for swiping questions can be treated as another keystroke. The questions and choices will be saved in an array-like structure after fetching it from the database. So, to respond to the swipe, I simply show another question from another index by iterating it (using Java API collections of course). These two functionalities (swiping questions and choosing answer) are just the basic. So, in further development we can have many more features to implement, such as checking unanswered question, see remaining time, zoom-in and zoom-out (will be used to check the detail of some pictures in the question), and many more!

That is a very simple interface for CBT that I made. It doesn't have to be an Applet, what matters is it has to be capable of receiving keystroke. If it doesn't work (no mechanism to receive keystroke directly from P5 Glove action like I mentioned before), then I have to think another way to do this or simply use normal settings available in P5 Glove (move cursors on screen and bend index finger to click the radio button).

Above I show you a little piece of Java source code on how the system will respond to keystroke received. It is still not in detail because like I told you before I don't know whether this keystroke will work on normal P5 Virtual Glove. But, it will definitely works if I use modified glove for gaming, like Peregrine.

I suppose that's all (enough for long explanation) for the technical explanation on the implementation. Let's discuss a little about the non-technical implementation. Maybe, I would start to implement this on my college, ITB, because this place is where I study and run my activities and of course because there are gloves available on GaIB Lab (GaIB Lab stands for Computer Graphics and Artificial Intelligence Laboratorium). It is hard to implement this on large scale as you know the glove is expensive and ITB has only limited resource. So, the suitable usage would be inside my college (small scale). It can be used for SBMPTN(student entry test which is held annually) or TOEFL test (held several times a year) in ITB or maybe for other similar occations.
